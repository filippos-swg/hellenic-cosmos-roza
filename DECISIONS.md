## Decision Log

### 2026-05-25 — Pilot Hierarchy: Oracle Leads, Loom Supports

**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis / South North
**Status:** RECOMMENDED — pending Filippos sign-off

**Decision:**
Ask the Oracle is the lead Phase 1 pilot. The Living Loom is the support pilot.

**Rationale:**
The Oracle wins on: clarity (visitor proposition is instantly legible), emotional immediacy (atmospheric, personal, mysterious), visual drama (strong slide, strong photography), and pitch urgency (AI-culture intersection is the most fundable and culturally resonant intersection right now). The Loom is the stronger long-term installation but harder to communicate in a 30-second pitch moment.

The pairing is deliberately complementary — Oracle (myth / AI / individual / uncertainty) balances Loom (craft / material / collective / memory). Both remain in Phase 1 concept development.

**Not decided:** Which pilot develops into a full Phase 2 proposal. Recommendation: develop both further before choosing.

### 2026-05-25 — Pitch Argument Spine

**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis / South North
**Status:** RECOMMENDED — pending Filippos review

**Decision:**
The pitch follows a single narrative spine: Culture → Gap → Connection → System → Proof.

The pitch opens on cultural necessity (civilization keeps moving, meaning beats technology), not on institutional diagnosis. The gap (fragmentation) arrives second, after the bigger idea is established. Connection is the solution (not reinvention — coherence). The system (six pillars, annual themes) makes it structural. The proof (pilots, prototype, identity) makes it tangible.

This spine is implicit in the v03 deck but not named anywhere in canon. Naming it prevents drift across deck iterations.

### 2026-05-25 — Primary Project Intent

**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis / South North
**Status:** RECOMMENDED — pending Filippos confirmation

**Decision:**
Phase 1 is a strategic persuasion exercise, not a design brief.

The primary objective is to earn HC's institutional commitment to a new worldview. Brand direction, website prototype and pilot concepts are evidence in service of that argument — not the argument itself. If the identity work leads before the strategic argument lands, HC will evaluate it as execution quality rather than direction quality.

**Practical consequence:** Brand workstream (Phase 1A) should not be framed to the client as "brand work." It should be framed as "making the direction visible."

### 2026-05-25 — Working Platform Adopted

**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis / South North
**Status:** RECOMMENDED — working platform, not final approved copy

**Decision:**
Adopt "Civilization on the Move / Πολιτισμός εν Κινήσει" as the working strategic platform for Hellenic Cosmos.

**Why:**
Frames civilization as alive and moving, not preserved. Works in English and Greek. Avoids the "ancient meets future" cliché. Gives permission for technology, craft, migration, memory, language and future culture simultaneously.

**Caveat:**
Requires testing with native Greek copy sensitivity, brand identity and institutional tone before final confirmation.

### 2026-05-25 — Strategic Positioning: Own a Distinct Lane

**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis / South North
**Status:** RECOMMENDED

**Decision:**
Position Hellenic Cosmos as a future-facing cultural institution where Greek civilization is a living system. Not a venue with improved marketing.

Competitive lane: not Onassis (contemporary cultural intelligence), not SNFCC (civic infrastructure). Own: Greek civilization as a living cultural system — history, myth, science, democracy, theatre, philosophy, navigation, language, craft — not as archives but as active cultural forces.

**Why:**
The existing ingredients are strong. The failure is connection, not content. The work is not reinvention — it is making the ecosystem visible as a single coherent worldview.

### 2026-05-25 — Brand Move: Evolution, Not Erasure

**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis / South North
**Status:** RECOMMENDED

**Decision:**
Keep the existing four-square logo logic. Rebuild the brand system around it, not over it.

The four-square grid becomes the foundation for: navigation, signage, programme categories, exhibition identity, motion behaviour and digital UI. The mark evolves from static badge to living symbolic grid.

**Alternatives considered:**
Full rebrand / new mark.

**Why:**
The existing logo contains useful institutional DNA — modular grid, symbolic system, Greek references, recognisable public memory. The problem is dated execution, not the concept.
