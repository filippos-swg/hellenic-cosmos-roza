# Creative Direction

**Project:** Project Roza / Hellenic Cosmos
**Owner:** Filippos Arvanitakis
**Last updated:** 2026-05-25

---

## Creative Position

> Ancient intelligence becoming active again.

The visual identity should make the movement of Greek civilization visible. Not a costume change. Not a campaign refresh. The feeling: a cultural system that has always been in motion, now made legible.

---

## Design System: The Living System

Four recurring visual behaviours that form the identity engine:

**Grid** — from looms, maps, architecture, city plans, code, screens and exhibition systems.
**Fragment** — from ruins, objects, texts, letters, memories and archival material.
**Route** — from Odyssey, migration, trade, sea, navigation and cultural movement.
**Signal** — from projection, light, sound, data, interaction and digital systems.

Grid + Fragment + Route + Signal — these are not aesthetic choices. They are structural. They define how the brand behaves across all surfaces.

---

## Four-Square Grid Logic

The existing four-square logo is not discarded. It is unlocked.

The grid becomes the foundation of the whole brand system:
- Master brand mark
- Programme navigation
- Icon system
- Website menu logic
- Signage system
- Exhibition identity framework
- Motion graphics
- Social templates
- Tickets and printed material
- Cultural experiment interface

The mark evolves from static badge to living symbolic grid.

## Four Quadrant Symbols

**Recommended working version:**

1. **Ideas** — philosophy, language, mathematics, thought
2. **Craft** — making, architecture, weaving, tools, technique
3. **Polis** — democracy, theatre, public life, education, gathering
4. **Cosmos** — sea, routes, navigation, myth, sky, systems

**Alternative (Greek terminology):**
Logos / Techne / Polis / Cosmos

**Status:** Ideas / Craft / Polis / Cosmos is recommended — more accessible, less academically heavy. Final decision requires client conversation and identity development.

## Colour Direction

Avoid predictable national blue and white as the primary system.

**Core palette:**
- Carbon black
- Stone white
- Deep Aegean blue
- Warm bronze
- Mineral grey

**Optional accents:**
- Crimson / agency red
- Oxblood
- Papyrus
- Olive
- Clay
- Signal gold

The system should support both calm institutional use and high-impact exhibition campaigns.

---

## Typography Direction

**Primary** — refined contemporary serif or high-craft display serif.
Purpose: authority, culture, intelligence.

**Secondary** — sharp grotesk / neo-grotesk / humanist sans.
Purpose: navigation, UI, programme information, tickets.

**Feeling:** Classical intelligence + contemporary precision.

Avoid:
- Fake ancient Greek fonts
- Generic tech fonts
- Overly friendly educational sans-serif

---

## Tone of Voice

**Is:**
intelligent / clear / contemporary / warm / culturally confident / precise / poetic only when useful / slightly mysterious / generous

**Is not:**
ministry language / tourist language / school-museum language / over-academic / innovation jargon / bureaucratic / inflated

**The shift:**

Instead of: "Discover the fascinating world of ancient Greece."
Say: "Civilization is not still. It moves through language, memory, conflict, craft, technology and imagination."

---

## Motion Identity

Motion is not decorative. "Civilization on the Move" demands it.

Behaviours:
- Symbols assemble and separate
- Routes connect across maps
- Letters form and dissolve
- Patterns weave in real time
- Fragments become systems
- Light scans across surfaces
- Grids open and close
- Orbital and cosmic movement

Motion feeling: intelligent and calm. Not hyperactive.

---

## Imagery Direction

Three modes:

**Artefact as Alive** — cinematic close-ups, fragments, inscriptions, sculpture, material
**People in Experience** — visitors inside light, projection, sound, conversation, learning
**Cultural Systems** — maps, routes, patterns, looms, diagrams, constellations, letterforms, data

Avoid:
- Stock families smiling at exhibits
- Flat building photography
- Generic ancient ruins
- Tourist postcard Greece
- Excessive marble nostalgia

---

## Anti-References

What this must never look or feel like:
- Tourist Greece
- Lazy blue and white nationalism
- Fake ancient fonts
- Generic tech startup
- Museum beige
- Overly patriotic visual language
- School museum
- Fortune-teller aesthetic (critical for Oracle work)
- Cute folklore / craft fair aesthetic (critical for Loom work)
