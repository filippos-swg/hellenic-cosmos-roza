## Pressure-Test Criteria

Work through each of the following. Flag what holds, what needs sharpening, what is contradictory, what is missing.

1. **Worldview** — Does "Civilization on the Move" hold as a belief, not a slogan? Is it true in a way that is distinctive and defensible?
2. **Institutional diagnosis** — Is the fragmentation argument accurate and fair? Does it land without feeling like an attack on HC's existing work?
3. **Pitch spine** — Does Culture → Gap → Connection → System → Proof hold in sequence? Does each step earn the next?
4. **Strategic framing** — Is the competitive lane (vs Onassis, vs SNFCC) credible? Is HC's claimed ownership of "civilization as living system" actually available to them?
5. **Narrative clarity** — Can a board member who has never heard of Hellenic Cosmos follow the argument in one read? Where does it lose them?
6. **Emotional strength** — Where does the pitch move? Where is it flat? What is the single most emotionally resonant moment?
7. **Board/client legibility** — Is the institutional benefit clear? Revenue, funding, audience, sponsorship, positioning — does the pitch connect direction to institutional value?
8. **Sponsor logic** — Is the sponsorship opportunity legible? Does the ecosystem model make the pitch fundable?
9. **Strategic contradictions** — Are there internal contradictions? Promises the concept portfolio cannot deliver? Claims that require capabilities HC may not have?
10. **Scope discipline** — Is Phase 1 tight enough? Is the ask (from HC) proportionate to the proof being offered?
